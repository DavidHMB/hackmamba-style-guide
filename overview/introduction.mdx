This guide is for technical writers. It covers documentation for developer-facing products, open source tools, and API-first platforms.

It combines every rule, principle, and standard from the Hackmamba Technical Writer Course, Professional Experience with teams, and client engagements. It assumes you already write in Markdown, use Git for version control, and have at least one documentation project under your belt.

If a situation isn't covered here, check the [Google Developer Documentation Style Guide](https://developers.google.com/style). If it's still unclear, choose the option that's easier to read, scan, and understand.

## New to this guide?

Start with the principles before you read the rules. Understanding why documentation works the way it does makes the specific rules easier to apply and remember.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the six documentation standards">
    These are the six criteria every piece of documentation must meet before it's ready to publish. Everything else in this guide flows from them.

    [Read the six standards →](/overview/six-standards)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work through the principles">
    The principles section explains the thinking behind good documentation: how developers read, how to reduce cognitive load, how to structure content so users can find what they need.

    [Start with user-focused writing →](/principles/user-focused)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learn the writing rules">
    Once you understand the principles, the rules make sense rather than feeling arbitrary.

    [Start with voice, tense, and person →](/writing-rules/voice-tense-person)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the editing checklist to review your work">
    Before every submission, run your content through the editing checklist. It maps every rule in this guide to one of the six documentation standards.

    [Go to the editing checklist →](/reference/editing-checklist)
  </Step>
</Steps>
