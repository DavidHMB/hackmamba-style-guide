---
title: "How-to guides"
description: "How to structure a task-focused guide for users who already know the basics."
---

A how-to guide is goal-oriented. It assumes the reader already has experience with the product and wants to accomplish a specific task. It doesn't teach. It guides.

## Required page structure

Every how-to guide must follow this structure. Each section is required.

| Section | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Introduction** | State what the reader will accomplish. Be specific about the outcome. |
| **Prerequisites** | List what the reader needs before starting. Include access requirements and versions. |
| **Steps** | Numbered list. One action per step. Describe the visible result after steps that produce one. |

<Note>
How-to guides don't need a Next steps section unless the page is part of a sequence where the next step isn't obvious from the platform navigation.
</Note>

## Introduction

State the outcome directly. Don't describe the feature or provide background.

<CodeGroup>
```text Right
This guide shows you how to connect your Slack workspace to receive alert notifications.
```

```text Wrong
Slack is a messaging platform. Integration allows you to receive notifications.
```
</CodeGroup>

## Prerequisites

List everything the reader needs before starting.

```text
Before you begin:
- You need admin access to your account.
- You need a Slack workspace with permission to install apps.
- The integration feature must be enabled on your plan.
```

## Steps

One action per step. Describe the visible result immediately after any step that produces one.

```text
1. Select **Settings** in the left sidebar.

2. Select **Integrations**, then select **Add integration**.

   The integrations panel opens.

3. Select **Slack** from the list of available integrations.

4. Enter your Slack workspace URL in the **Workspace URL** field.

5. Select **Connect**.

   A confirmation message appears and the Slack integration status changes to **Active**.
```

## Assumptions

A how-to guide assumes the reader:
- Has already completed the getting started guide
- Has basic familiarity with the product
- Knows what they're trying to accomplish and needs the steps, not the context

If the reader needs background before they can follow the steps, link to the relevant concept page or tutorial. Don't put explanation inside a how-to guide.
